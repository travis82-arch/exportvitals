App compatibility and setup

Why won’t the Oura app install on my Android phone?

If Google Play says the Oura app is not compatible with your device, the most common reason is that the phone does not meet the current app requirements. Oura currently lists Android 10 or newer, Google Play services, and Bluetooth 4.0 support as Android requirements.

Updating the Google Play Store or Google Play services may help with some app problems, but it does not upgrade an Android 9 phone to Android 10. If the phone’s operating system is too old, Google Play may continue to block installation.

What Android version does the Oura app require?

Oura currently lists Android 10 or newer with Google Play services as the Android requirement for the Oura app. The phone must also support Bluetooth 4.0.

This requirement can change, so the official Oura support page should be treated as the source of truth for final compatibility decisions.

Bluetooth, syncing, and desktop workarounds

Can I sync my Oura Ring directly to a Windows PC?

Not through a normal official desktop workflow. A Windows PC may have Bluetooth, but Oura does not provide a standard Windows app that pairs with the ring, retrieves the raw ring data, syncs it to your Oura account, and prepares it for export.

The normal path is: Oura Ring → official Oura mobile app → Oura account/cloud → official export tools.

Exporting and viewing your data

Does ExportVitals upload my Oura ZIP?

No. ExportVitals is designed around a local-first workflow. Your supported export file is parsed in your browser rather than uploaded to an ExportVitals server for parsing.

This is one of the main reasons ExportVitals exists: many users want a readable dashboard without sending private wearable exports through another cloud service.

Can ExportVitals sync directly with my Oura Ring?

No. ExportVitals does not connect to your ring over Bluetooth, does not access your Oura account, and does not pull live data from Oura services.

It only works with files you already exported through official Oura tools, then imported locally into your browser.

Can ExportVitals help if my phone is too old for the Oura app?

It can help you read and explore exported Oura data on a desktop browser after export, but it does not replace the supported mobile app workflow required for ring syncing.

If your phone cannot run the Oura app, you may still need a compatible device to sync the ring and generate fresh export data first.

Can ExportVitals replace Oura on the Web?

No. ExportVitals is an unofficial local viewer for exported files and is not a replacement for Oura’s official app, account, subscription features, or web services.

Use it as a complementary analysis view once you already have a downloaded export ZIP.

What should I do first: sync, export, or import into ExportVitals?

Use this order: sync your ring in the official Oura app, export data through Oura’s official export flow, then import the ZIP into ExportVitals.

Importing older exports is fine, but you will only see what was included in the exported files.
